# Footer

The Footer component provides a structured area at the bottom of the page to display essential information, navigational links, branding, and utility actions. It supports customizable slots for content and branding, aligning with gov.ie standards and accessibility requirements.

## Structure

The Gov.ie Design System Footer is structured into three key sections:

- **Primary Slot**: Used for navigational links, summaries, or descriptive blocks.
- **Secondary Slot**: Contains the official logo and optional secondary content like metadata or partner links.
- **Utility Slot**: Typically used for legal links such as privacy policy, accessibility statement, etc.

## Usage

Footers are used to provide consistent structure and reliable access to persistent links or brand identity at the bottom of each page.

### Do

- **Use all relevant slots**: Include meaningful, well-organized content in `primarySlot` and `secondarySlot` to improve structure and navigation.
- **Use a grid layout or clear alignment**: Ensure items in `primarySlot` and `secondarySlot` are visually balanced and easy to scan.
- **Group related links or information**: Organize content into columns or sections to support usability.

### Don't

- **Don’t place unstructured content**: Avoid dumping unrelated or ungrouped items into the footer.
- **Don’t use inconsistent alignment**: Keep column widths and content hierarchy aligned for readability.
- **Don’t overload the footer with excessive links**: Prioritize key navigation and legal content, not every page.
- **Don’t forget spacing and layout**: Crowded or overlapping content negatively impacts both accessibility and design clarity.
