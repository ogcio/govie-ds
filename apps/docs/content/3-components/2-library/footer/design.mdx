# Footer

The Footer provides a structured area at the bottom of the page for essential information, navigational links, branding, and utility actions. It is assembled from composable primitives - `Footer`, `FooterSection`, and `FooterLogo` - so you control layout while keeping consistent Gov.ie styling and accessibility.

## Structure

Compose the Footer from these building blocks:

- **`Footer`**: The landmark (`<footer>`) that wraps all sections.
- **`FooterSection` (primary)**: The main content area. Use it for navigational link columns, secondary links, social icons, and the logo. Separate primary and secondary content with a divider when both are present.
- **`FooterSection` (utility)**: A distinct lower band for legal links such as privacy policy, accessibility statement, and copyright.
- **`FooterLogo`**: Positions the official logo beside secondary content. Place your logo (for example `LogoGoldGreen`) as its children.

Use `Container`, `Grid`, `Stack`, and `Divider` inside sections to control layout - the Footer primitives do not impose a fixed grid.

## Usage

Footers provide consistent structure and reliable access to persistent links and
brand identity at the bottom of each page. Include only the sections your
service needs - a complete primary layout, secondary links with a logo, or
utility content alone.

### Do

- **Compose with clear sections**: Use a primary `FooterSection` for navigation and branding, and a utility `FooterSection` for legal and copyright content.
- **Use a grid or clear alignment**: Organise link columns with `Grid` or `Stack` so content is visually balanced and easy to scan.
- **Group related links**: Keep related links under shared headings and separate primary and secondary content with a `Divider` when both are present.
- **Place the logo with `FooterLogo`**: Keep the official logo beside secondary links, not buried inside unstructured content.

<Footer>
  <FooterSection>
    <Container className="">
      <FooterSecondaryContent />
    </Container>
  </FooterSection>
  <FooterSection variant="utility">
    <FooterUtilityContent />
  </FooterSection>
</Footer>

### Don't

- **Don’t place unstructured content**: Avoid dumping unrelated or ungrouped items into a section without headings, columns, or spacing.
- **Don’t use inconsistent alignment**: Keep column widths and hierarchy aligned so the footer is scannable.
- **Don’t overload the footer with excessive links**: Prioritise key navigation and legal content, not every page on the site.
- **Don’t forget spacing and layout**: Crowded or overlapping content harms accessibility and clarity - use `Container`, `Divider`, and `Stack`/`Grid` thoughtfully.
